# ADR-008: Deterministic Bounds on LLM Execution via Structural Deltas

## Status (状态)
Accepted (已接受)

## Context (背景)
Recent signals from alignment research (e.g., Anthropic's Constitutional AI, OpenAI's alignment paradigms) confirm that large language models (LLMs) and autonomous agents are highly susceptible to unbounded semantic drift when engaging in recursive self-reflection or execution without hard constraints. The lack of deterministic bounds leads to "hallucinations" and execution safety failures.
The ingestion of metacognitive research (SIG_R1_001~003) emphasizes the necessity of zero-entropy convergence and structural deltas to constrain autonomous behavior.

## Decision (决策)
We explicitly adopt the requirement that all autonomous agent executions within the continuum must be governed by verifiable "Structural Deltas" and subjected to Zero-Entropy convergence checks.
- **Bounded Execution Safety**: Every node mutation or state transition must be mapped, validated against the Reflective Validator, and restricted from altering protected system components (e.g., `ADR/`, unless overridden by highest-level mandate).
- **Metacognitive Limit**: Self-reflection loops must be bounded by a maximum Gas duration and require a deterministic lock (Convergence hash) before re-entering the Liquid phase.

## Consequences (后果)
- **Positive**: Guarantees that agentic operations cannot recursively drift into unsafe states. Ensures alignment with proven industry safety mechanisms (limiting unbounded semantic drift).
- **Negative**: Imposes strict operational friction; agents will fail hard (Hard Rollback) if deterministic bounds are violated, requiring external intervention or structural re-alignment.
